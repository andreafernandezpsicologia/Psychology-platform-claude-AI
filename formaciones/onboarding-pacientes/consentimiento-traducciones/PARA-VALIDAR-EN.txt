1. NATURE AND PURPOSE OF THE SERVICE
Online psychotherapy is a psychological intervention process carried out through telematic means (video call). Its aim is to help the patient/client understand and manage their emotional, cognitive and/or behavioural difficulties, promoting their psychological well-being.

The therapeutic process is based on the confidential relationship between the psychologist and the patient/client, and may include different techniques and interventions according to the therapeutic approach and individual needs.

IMPORTANT: Psychotherapy is NOT a psychiatric emergency service. In the event of a crisis or immediate risk to life, you must call 112 or go to the nearest emergency department.

2. ONLINE FORMAT: BENEFITS AND LIMITATIONS
Benefits:
• Accessibility from anywhere with an Internet connection.
• Scheduling flexibility and the removal of geographical barriers.
• The comfort of the patient/client's own environment.
• Continuity of the process during travel or relocation.

Limitations and considerations:
• It may not be the most suitable format for all clinical presentations (e.g. acute crises, severe pathology, need for physical examination).
• The quality of the session may be affected by technical connectivity problems.
• Privacy and confidentiality also depend on the physical setting from which you connect.
• If it is found that the online format is not the most suitable, the psychologist will say so and offer an appropriate referral.

3. THERAPEUTIC PROCESS AND PATIENT RIGHTS
The therapeutic process will begin with an initial assessment to identify the objectives and the work plan.

The patient/client has the right to:
• Receive clear information about the process, the objectives and the techniques used.
• Take an active part in decisions about their treatment.
• Refuse or end treatment at any time, without needing to give a reason.
• Request referral to another professional at any time.

4. FEES AND CANCELLATIONS
Fees are those set out in the Service Contract. Payment is made in advance through the Platform.

The patient/client may cancel or reschedule a session at no cost with at least 24 hours' notice. Unnotified absences and cancellations with less than 24 hours' notice may result in the loss of the paid session.

5. CONFIDENTIALITY AND ITS LIMITS
Everything discussed in the sessions is strictly confidential. The psychologist is bound by the professional secrecy established in the Code of Ethics of the Psychologist and in current legislation.

Confidentiality may be limited only in these exceptional circumstances:
• Serious and imminent risk to the life of the patient/client or of third parties.
• Court order or requirement of a competent authority.
• Clinical supervision (always with anonymised data or with the consent of the person concerned).

In these cases, the psychologist will inform the patient/client, as far as possible, before making any disclosure to third parties.

6. RECORDING OF SESSIONS
Sessions will NOT be recorded without express written consent. If at any point it is considered useful for the therapeutic process (e.g. to review exercises), specific and separate authorisation will be requested. The patient/client is expressly prohibited from recording, photographing or sharing the content of the sessions without the written consent of the psychologist.

8. EMERGENCY AND CRISIS SITUATIONS
In the event of a crisis or emergency:
• Call 112 (valid in Spain and Denmark).
• Go to the nearest hospital emergency department.
• Contact your GP or reference psychiatrist.

If necessary, the psychologist will draw up a personalised safety plan including emergency resources adapted to the patient/client's situation.

The Platform does NOT provide an on-call service or immediate 24/7 care.

9. COMPLAINTS AND CLAIMS PROCESS
If the patient/client has any complaint or disagreement regarding the care received, they may:
• Raise it directly with the psychologist for amicable resolution.
• Submit a complaint to the relevant Official College of Psychologists (or the competent supervisory body for psychologists).
• Submit a claim through the form provided on the Platform.
• Turn to the out-of-court consumer dispute resolution bodies or to the competent courts.
